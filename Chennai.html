Chennai

Chennai, formerly called Madras, is a metropolitan city in Tamil Nadu, South India. It has a similar climate to Mumbai as it is also a coastal city. The major difference is its location—Chennai is on the eastern coast of India, while Mumbai is on the western coast. Chennai experiences a hot, humid, and tropical climate, which means it can rain anytime, offering relief from the heat but also increasing humidity. Tamil is the primary language spoken here, and the city was officially established in 1639.

If you want to travel to Chennai by Air India, it will take 2 hours and 5 minutes. However, traveling by train takes longer—22 hours on the CSMT Chennai Express.

The best time to visit Chennai is from November to February. Note that Chennai experiences heavy rains, so it’s advisable to carry an umbrella. Summers (March to May) can be extremely hot and humid, while the monsoon months (June to October) bring heavy rainfall, which may hinder travel plans.

Here are some popular tourist places in Chennai:

  1. Marina Beach: Similar to Juhu Beach in Mumbai, Marina Beach is a sandy beach established in 1889. During summer, it attracts 15,000–20,000 visitors daily, and up to 50,000 visitors on average. Note: Bathing and swimming are prohibited due to the strong sea waves.
  2. Kapaleeswarar Temple: A Hindu temple dedicated to Lord Shiva, built by the Pallavas and later rebuilt during the Vijayanagara era. Located in Mylapore, it features Dravidian architecture and hosts festivals like the Panguni Peruvila and Arupathimoovar festival.
  3. Guindy National Park
  4. Express Avenue
  5. Arignar Anna Zoological Park

Educational places in Chennai include B.M. Birla Planetarium, Periyar Science and Technology Centre, Victoria Public Hall, Government Museum Chennai, and Fort St. George Museum.
