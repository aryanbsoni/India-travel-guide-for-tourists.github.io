Kolkata

Kolkata is India's 4th and the last largest metropolitan city. Kolkata is located on the North-Eastern coast of India. It is situated in the state of West Bengal (Poschim Bongol) and on the coast of the Bay of Bengal. The city primarily speaks Bengali. It was formerly known as Calcutta, but after India's Independence in 1947, the government changed its name to Kolkata. It is also the capital of West Bengal.

During the British Raj, Calcutta was the capital of British India. Kolkata was discovered between the 14th and 16th centuries. The World Health Organization refers to this place as a "Megacity." Kolkata has a similar climate to other coastal cities like Mumbai, Chennai, and Visakhapatnam. The city experiences hot and humid weather, typical of a tropical climate, with frequent rains during summer and monsoon seasons.

By Train: Traveling by train takes longer, approximately 30 hours and 30 minutes. Travel to Kolkata by train

The best time to visit Kolkata is during the winter months (December to February) when the weather is cool and pleasant. The summer months (March to June) are hot and humid, while the monsoon season (June to November) can bring heavy rains and flooding.

Some popular tourist places to visit in Kolkata include:

  1. Victoria Memorial - A large marble building dedicated to Queen Victoria, built between 1906 and 1921. It is one of the largest monuments to a monarch in the world.
  2. Howrah Bridge - A cantilever bridge over the Hooghly River, built in 1942, and one of the most iconic landmarks of Kolkata.
