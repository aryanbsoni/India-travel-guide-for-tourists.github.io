Kolkata

Kolkata is India's 4th and the last largest metropolitian city. Kolkata is located on the North-Eastern coast of India. It is located on the coast of Bay of Bengal. It is situated in the state West Bengal (Poschim Bongol). In this city the language is Bengali. It was formely known as Calcutta but after the India's Independance year that is 1947 the government changed the name to Kolkata. Kolkata is also the capital of West Bengal. During the British Raj Era Calcutta was the capital of British India. Kolkata was discovered during the era of 14th to the 16th centuary. By the WHO it is said that this place is also a very large and a huge city which is called as Megacity . Kolkata has a similar climate like the coastal cities like Mumbai, Chennai, Visakhaptnam etc. This place is also hot and a humid place. Kolkata follows a tropical climate which means it rains anytime during summers and monsoon. It also has a dry climate like Mumbai as it does not rain during winter.

The best time to visit Kolkata is during winters but be alert of rainfall. The best time to visit Kolkata is during winters is because during the months of March to June that is the summers they are very hot and humid which will make you feel uncomfortable, then comes the monsoon months that is June to November which can be very heavy and flooding which might disturb your travelling. So, the best month is December to February that is the winter season since it is pleasently cool.

There are many tourist places to visit in Kolkata some are-

  1. Victoria Memorial- Victoria Memorial is a large marble building on the Maidan in central Kolkata. The place has its entrance on the Queen Victoria. The place was built during the British Era that is 1906 to 1921. Since it is a Memorial place it is given to the memory of the Queen Victoria who was the Empress of India during the British Era. This place is said to be the largest monument to a monarch {king} anywhere in this world. The another fact is that this museum is under the control of the Ministry of Culture. Victoria Memorial was constructed by architect Willam Emerson. In this place there are multiple gardens which you can often visit.
  2. Howarh Bridge- Howrah Bridge is a balanced cantilever bridge over the Hooghly river in West Bengali. It was consturctred by 1942 and the Bridge was open by 1943 so in simple terms people started to visit the bridge by the year 1943. When the bridge was new, it was named as New Howrah Bridge. On 14 June 1965 the bridge was renamed as Rabindra Setu . The name was inspired by Shri.Rabindranath Tagore a famous Bengali poet. The bridge is one of four on the Hoogly River and it is said that it is also a famous symbol of Kolkata and West Bengal. This Bridge is also the sixth-longest bridge in this world. During the era of 2000s it is said that the bridge had to go through a maintance since it became an old bridge by then. People often visit this place because it is a very beautiful bridge located in Kolkata.
